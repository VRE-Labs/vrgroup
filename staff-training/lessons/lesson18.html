Lesson 18: Understanding the Bouncer/Buffer Role

Learn how to screen users and ensure event security as part of the Bouncer/Buffer role.

📌 Overview

The Bouncer/Buffer role is a rotational sub-role assigned to Event Hosts, Moderators, Leads, and Officers. Its purpose is to screen users before they participate in an event, ensuring that all attendees meet the necessary criteria, such as age, trust levels, and verification.

This role rotates per event, so no single staff member carries the burden alone. Staff can opt to pass on the role, but passes are limited to ensure fairness.

🎯 Responsibilities of a Bouncer/Buffer

✅ Screen Users Before Entry

  • Check age requirements (if applicable).
  • Verify trust levels and community standing.
  • Ensure the participant meets event-specific requirements.

✅ Prevent Disruptions Before They Start

  • Watch for potential trolls, bad actors, or unverified users.
  • Deny access to anyone who does not meet event security standards.
  • Flag potential problems for the moderation team.

✅ Act as the First Point of Contact

  • If a user has questions about eligibility, the Bouncer/Buffer handles it.
  • In case of security concerns, they alert higher staff immediately.
  • They provide a smooth and secure transition for participants into the event.

🔄 Role Rotation & Pass System

To balance the workload, this role rotates each event, ensuring fairness.

  • All Event Hosts, Moderators, Leads, and Officers are eligible for the role.

Pass Limits:

  • 🔹 Moderators & Event Hosts: 2 passes per week / 6 per month.
  • 🔹 Leads & Officers: 1 pass per month.

If a staff member exceeds their pass limit, they must take the role when assigned.

🚨 When to Deny Entry?

Users should not be allowed to participate if they:

  • ❌ Do not meet the age or trust level requirements.
  • ❌ Show signs of trolling, ban evasion, or previous disruptions.
  • ❌ Refuse verification or attempt to bypass security.

If unsure, consult a Lead or Officer before making a final decision.

🎓 Training Task

Confirm your understanding of the Bouncer/Buffer role by answering the following:

  1. What are three key responsibilities of a Bouncer/Buffer?
  2. What are the pass limits for Moderators & Event Hosts?
  3. What should you do if you’re unsure whether to allow a user in?
  4. Who should you report to if an issue escalates beyond screening?

⚡ Submit your answers to a Lead or Officer to complete this training!
