Lesson 23: Understanding How to Read, Fill Out, and Submit a F.L.A.R.E Report

Learn how to effectively document events using the F.L.A.R.E Report for continuous improvement.

1. What is a F.L.A.R.E Report?

The F.L.A.R.E Report is a post-event documentation form designed to capture Feedback, Learnings, Attendance, Reflection, and Enhancements. It's a structured way for staff to evaluate how an event went and ensure continual improvement in community engagement and event hosting.

2. When Is It Required?

  • After every official event hosted within the community.
  • Filled out by the primary host or acting moderator.
  • Must be signed off by a Lead or Officer, unless that was the person hosting.

3. Breakdown of Each Section

🔥 F – Feedback

  • General atmosphere & vibe
  • Guest reactions & interactions
  • Any received feedback (screenshots, quotes, DMs)

💡 L – Learnings

  • What worked well
  • Fun or engaging moments
  • Recognition received

👥 A – Attendance

  • Estimated turnout
  • Peak hours
  • How long guests stayed

🔍 R – Reflection

  • Weak points or trouble areas
  • Logistical or technical issues
  • Did staffing impact the event?

🚀 E – Enhancements

  • Would you run this event again?
  • What would be done differently?
  • Suggestions for improvement

4. How to Fill Out the Report

  1. Open the designated form or document template.
  2. Enter all required event information and answer each section honestly and thoroughly.
  3. Include screenshots or quotes if relevant.
  4. Ensure it is co-signed by the Lead/Officer (or self-sign if applicable).
  5. Submit it in the appropriate staff channel or shared drive (as directed).

5. Reminders

  • F.L.A.R.E Reports are required to maintain transparency and growth in our community’s events.
  • If you are ever unsure, ask a Lead or Officer for guidance!
