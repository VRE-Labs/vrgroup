Lesson 14: Scheduling Guidelines for VREvents

Learn the principles and best practices for scheduling engaging and well-balanced events for the VREvents community.

Event Scheduling Basics

  • Allowed Days: Events can be scheduled from Tuesday to Saturday only.
  • Rest Days: Sundays and Mondays are designated rest daysâ€”no events should be scheduled on these days.
  • Time Window: Events must be scheduled between 6:00 PM - 11:45 PM Mountain Time (MT) / 7:00 PM - 12:00 AM Central Time (CT).
  • One Event Per Day: Only one event per day is allowed to prevent overlap and ensure quality.
  • World Limit: Each event may include up to four different worlds but should be cohesive in theme or structure.

Event Frequency and Spacing

  • Weekly Limit: The maximum number of scheduled events per week is four (4).
  • Avoid Back-to-Back Events: Prevent scheduling events back-to-back-to-back to avoid staff burnout and maintain community engagement.
  • Spread Events: Distribute events throughout the week to maximize participation and maintain excitement.

Scheduling Best Practices

  • Diversity of Events: Rotate between different event styles (e.g., social, horror, competitive, mini-games) to keep the experience fresh.
  • Check for Overlaps: Ensure events do not coincide with major VRChat updates, holidays, or other significant events that could impact attendance.
  • Promotion Timing: Schedule events with enough lead time for proper promotion (minimum 3-5 days in advance).
  • Staffing Considerations: Confirm moderator and host availability before finalizing an event schedule.
  • Peak Hours Awareness: Consider scheduling events during peak activity times within the designated scheduling window for maximum participation.

Conclusion

By adhering to these guidelines, we can maintain the high-quality, immersive experiences that define VREvents while ensuring a sustainable and engaging environment for both attendees and staff. Happy planning!
