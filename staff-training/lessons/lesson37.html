Lesson 37: P.A.W.S. Pause & Wellness Support

Promote Awareness, Wellness, and Support - Mental health awareness and burnout prevention for staff and members.

1. 🐾 What is P.A.W.S.?

The P.A.W.S. initiative is designed to promote mental health awareness and prevent burnout by:

  • Promoting Awareness: Educating staff and members about mental health and self-care.
  • Wellness Support: Providing resources and safe spaces for emotional well-being.
  • Encouraging Rest: Reminding everyone to take breaks and prioritize their health.

This initiative is built on compassion, not performance, and aims to create a supportive community environment.

2. 🛠️ How It Works

  • Chilldown Days: Scheduled days where no major events or tasks are planned, allowing everyone to relax.
  • Safe Vent Channels: Dedicated spaces for members to share feelings and seek support.
  • Wellness Reminders: Regular prompts to rest, hydrate, and practice self-care.
  • Resource Sharing: Provide links to mental health resources, hotlines, and articles.

3. 🎧 Staff Responsibilities

  • Encourage participation in wellness activities and discussions.
  • Monitor vent channels to ensure they remain safe and supportive.
  • Model healthy behaviors by taking breaks and practicing self-care.
  • Share resources and tips for managing stress and burnout.

4. 📋 P.A.W.S. Do’s and Don’ts

✅ Do:

  • Promote a culture of compassion and understanding.
  • Encourage open conversations about mental health.
  • Provide support without judgment or pressure.

❌ Don’t:

  • Dismiss or minimize someone’s feelings or concerns.
  • Pressure members or staff to participate in wellness activities.
  • Allow vent channels to become toxic or unmoderated.

5. 📎 Tracking Impact

To measure the success of the P.A.W.S. initiative, track:

  • Participation in wellness activities and discussions.
  • Feedback from staff and members about the initiative.
  • Overall community sentiment and engagement levels.

This ensures the initiative continues to foster a supportive and healthy community environment.
