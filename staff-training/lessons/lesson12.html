Lesson 12: Understanding How to Moderate Effectively in Minecraft

Learn the tools, commands, and best practices for moderating the VRE Minecraft SMP effectively.

📌 Introduction

Moderating a Minecraft server is different from moderating a Discord or VRChat event. In an open-world sandbox like Minecraft, players can break rules in more subtle ways, requiring moderators to be proactive, observant, and responsive. This guide will walk you through the key principles of effective moderation within the VRE Minecraft SMP.

🛠️ Tools & Commands

1️⃣ Player Monitoring & Management

  • /kick <player> <reason>: Removes a player from the server temporarily.
  • /ban <player> <reason>: Permanently bans a player. Use sparingly.
  • /tempban <player> <duration> <reason>: Issues a temporary ban.
  • /mute <player> <duration> <reason>: Prevents a player from chatting.
  • /unmute <player>: Lifts a mute.
  • /warn <player> <reason>: Officially warns a player, which gets logged.
  • /msg <player>: Sends a private message to a player.

2️⃣ Anti-Griefing & World Protection

  • /co inspect: Checks recent block activity (requires CoreProtect).
  • /rollback <player> <time>: Rolls back a specific player’s actions.
  • /region flag <region> <flag> <value>: Adjusts WorldGuard protections.
  • /vanish: Hides you from players for stealth moderation.

3️⃣ Chat & Behavior Monitoring

  • /chat clear: Clears the chat to remove spam or offensive content.
  • /mutechat: Temporarily disables global chat in extreme cases.
  • /socialspy: Allows staff to monitor private messages (use responsibly).

🚨 Common Issues & How to Handle Them

1️⃣ Griefing (Destroying Builds)

  • 📌 How to Handle:
  • Use /co inspect to check who broke/placed blocks.
  • Rollback the grief with /rollback <player> <time>.
  • If intentional and repeated, warn or temp-ban the player.
  • If unclear, speak with the player before taking action.

2️⃣ Hacking & Exploits (X-ray, Fly Hacks, Speed Hacks)

  • 📌 How to Handle:
  • Signs: Unnaturally fast movement, sudden large amounts of rare materials, suspicious activity.
  • Use /vanish to observe them undetected.
  • Gather evidence (screenshots or logs).
  • Issue a tempban or permanent ban based on severity.

3️⃣ Chat Violations (Harassment, Slurs, Spam, Advertising)

  • 📌 How to Handle:
  • Mild offenses: Issue a warning with /warn <player> <reason>.
  • Repeated offenses: Mute the player with /mute <player> <duration> <reason>.
  • Severe offenses: Tempban or permanently ban the player.
  • Advertising: Immediate mute and potential removal.

4️⃣ Rule Evasion (Ban/Mute Evasion, Alt Accounts)

  • 📌 How to Handle:
  • Check IPs (if applicable via staff plugins).
  • If a player rejoins under a new account to evade punishment, issue an IP ban.
  • Report to server leads for further action.

🎯 Best Practices for Minecraft Moderation

  • ✅ Stay Neutral & Professional: Never escalate arguments or use aggressive language.
  • ✅ Gather Evidence: Take screenshots or record logs before issuing serious punishments.
  • ✅ Communicate Clearly: Explain the reason for any action taken.
  • ✅ Use the Chain of Escalation: Always start with a warning unless the situation is severe.
  • ✅ Be Consistent: Ensure all moderators enforce rules uniformly.
  • ✅ Keep Logs: Always document bans, mutes, and major incidents for reference.

📢 Final Notes & Staff Expectations

  • All moderation actions should be justified and logged to prevent abuse.
  • Do not abuse your power (e.g., spawning items for yourself, favoring certain players).
