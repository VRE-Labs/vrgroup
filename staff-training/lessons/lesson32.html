Lesson 32: C.O.R.E. Development

Community Outreach, Retention & Enrichment - A strategic hub for planning events, announcements, and collaborations.

1. 🧠 What is C.O.R.E. Development?

The C.O.R.E. Development initiative serves as a tactical command center to:

  • Plan: Strategically map out seasonal events, major announcements, and collaborations.
  • Coordinate: Align efforts across teams to ensure smooth execution.
  • Enrich: Enhance community engagement through well-timed and impactful initiatives.

2. 🛠️ How It Works

  • Seasonal Planning: Outline key events and announcements for each quarter.
  • Collaboration: Work with leads and coordinators to align goals and resources.
  • Hype Cycles: Build excitement through teasers, previews, and countdowns.
  • Feedback Integration: Use community input to refine plans and ensure relevance.

3. 🎧 Staff Responsibilities

  • Participate in planning sessions to contribute ideas and insights.
  • Coordinate with other teams to ensure alignment and resource availability.
  • Track progress and adjust plans as needed to address challenges or opportunities.
  • Communicate plans clearly to the community to build excitement and engagement.

4. 📋 C.O.R.E. Do’s and Don’ts

✅ Do:

  • Set clear goals and timelines for each initiative.
  • Encourage collaboration and input from all relevant teams.
  • Use data and feedback to guide decision-making.

❌ Don’t:

  • Overload the calendar with too many events at once.
  • Ignore feedback or fail to adapt plans based on community needs.
  • Delay communication, which can lead to confusion or missed opportunities.

5. 📎 Tracking Impact

To measure the success of C.O.R.E. Development, track:

  • Engagement metrics for events and announcements.
  • Feedback from staff and community members about the planning process.
  • Overall alignment with community goals and values.

This ensures the initiative remains effective and continues to drive community growth and enrichment.
