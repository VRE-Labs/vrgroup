Lesson 7: Progress Performance Improvement Plans

Learn how to use structured improvement plans to enhance event quality and staff performance.

Introduction

A Progress Performance Improvement Plan (PPIP) is a structured approach designed to enhance the effectiveness of event planning, execution, and moderation. PPIPs are used to identify performance gaps, provide actionable feedback, and set clear goals for improvement. This ensures that VREvents maintains a high standard of quality and professionalism in all hosted events.

üîπ Step 1: Identify the Issue

  • Gather feedback from attendees, staff, and event analytics.
  • Determine if the issue is related to planning, execution, or moderation.
  • Clearly define the problem in objective terms.

üîπ Step 2: Develop an Improvement Plan

  • Set SMART Goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound).
  • Outline steps to reach those goals (e.g., better time management, improved moderation practices, clearer communication).
  • Assign responsibilities to the relevant individuals.

üîπ Step 3: Provide Support & Training

  • Ensure that staff have the necessary tools to succeed.
  • Encourage open communication and feedback throughout the process.

üîπ Step 4: Monitor Progress & Adjust as Needed

  • Make necessary adjustments if the plan needs refinement.
  • Document improvements and recognize achievements.

üîπ Step 5: Final Evaluation & Next Steps

  • Decide if further action is needed (e.g., additional training, reassignment, or recognition of progress).
