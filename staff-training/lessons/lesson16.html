Lesson 16: Understanding the VRChat-based VREvents FAQ

Learn how to assist members effectively by mastering the answers to frequently asked questions about VRChat-based events.

Objective

The goal of this lesson is to ensure that staff and leadership have a complete understanding of the frequently asked questions (FAQ) regarding VRChat-based events, server functionality, and community protocols for VREvents. This will help ensure staff can assist members effectively and uphold community guidelines.

Instructions

  • Read and Understand the FAQ: Carefully review the FAQ section below. Take note of how these answers contribute to community safety, event accessibility, and member engagement.
  • Practical Application: Simulate scenarios where a member asks a question, and practice explaining the answer in a clear, concise, and friendly manner.
  • Staff Check-in: After reviewing the FAQ, demonstrate your understanding by explaining specific answers to a colleague or supervisor.

FAQ Breakdown for Staff Training

How do I fix read-only channels?

Answer: Verify your VRChat account with the VRify bot by using the /verify command in DMs. This is required for all VRChat-based events to ensure security and a bot-free community.

Key Takeaway: Staff should guide members to complete the verification process and explain the importance of verifying their VRChat account for access.

Can I host my own event?

Answer: Yes, you can apply to be an Event Host. For more details, check the application channel or contact an Events Lead.

Key Takeaway: Staff should be ready to guide potential event hosts through the application process.

How do I report someone for breaking the rules?

Answer: Report to an Events Moderator or Events Lead through DMs or use the ticket reporting system in the server.

Key Takeaway: Staff should be familiar with the reporting process, including how to use the /ticket create command.

Do I need a VR headset to participate in events?

Answer: No, while it enhances the experience, many events are Quest and Desktop-friendly.

Key Takeaway: Staff should reassure members that events are accessible regardless of hardware, though a VR headset is encouraged.

How do I become a Moderator?

Answer: Apply through the Moderator Application process available in the applications channel or by contacting an Events Lead.

Key Takeaway: Staff should direct members interested in moderation roles to the correct application resources.

What are the rules for avatars in VREvents?

Answer: Avatars should be cross-compatible and not cause excessive visual or audio disruptions (e.g., flashing lights, loud sounds).

Key Takeaway: Staff must ensure that members are aware of avatar guidelines to prevent disruptions during events.

Answer: Yes, submit your event idea in the Event Suggestions channel or discuss it with an Events Host.

Key Takeaway: Staff should be open to suggestions and assist members in submitting ideas to improve the event offerings.

What happens if I break the rules?

Answer: Depending on the severity, you may face warnings, timeouts, kicks, or bans. The moderation system is progressive.

Key Takeaway: Staff should be aware of the progressive disciplinary process and ensure fair enforcement of the rules.

How do I report someone?

Answer: Use the /ticket create command to report issues. A moderator will review the case.

Key Takeaway: Staff should be proficient in helping members use the ticket system to report misconduct.

What do I do if someone is DM advertising?

Answer: Do not engage with the advertiser. Report them using /ticket create or DM a staff member, including evidence if possible.

Key Takeaway: Staff should stress the importance of reporting DM advertising promptly to maintain community standards.

Answer: Reach Level 20 in the server to unlock access to promotional channels. Level up by engaging in events and community discussions.
