Lesson 11: Understanding How Mutes Interact with the Verification System

Learn how the mute role affects user interactions with the verification system and how to manage muted users effectively.

Overview

When a user is muted, they are assigned a mute role flag, which limits their access to the server and restricts their ability to interact with channels, including the verification process.

How the Mute Role Works

  • Muted Role Assignment: Muted users are automatically assigned a Muted role.
  • Channel Restrictions: This role removes access to all channels except for #muted.
  • Communication in #muted: Muted users can only communicate in #muted, where they can be contacted by staff.

Impact on Verification

  • Permission Overrides: Since the muted role overrides regular permissions, muted users may not be able to complete standard verification steps.
  • Verification Commands: Muted users cannot access verification commands or interact with verification bots.
  • Manual Review: Staff must manually review muted users to determine if they should be unmuted or remain restricted.

Communicating with Muted Users

  • Use #muted: Muted users are expected to respond in #muted.
  • Staff Communication: Staff should use this channel to ask for clarification regarding why the mute was triggered and what steps the user needs to take.
  • Response Timeframe: Users who fail to respond within a set timeframe (6-12 hours) may be subject to further action, such as removal from the server.

Resolving a Mute

  • User Explanation: The muted user should explain their situation in #muted.
  • Staff Review: A moderator or officer evaluates their response and determines whether the mute was justified.
  • Action Taken: The mute is either removed (if deemed accidental or unjustified) or upheld (if rule violations persist).

Conclusion

By maintaining a structured approach to handling mutes, we ensure that legitimate members retain access while effectively managing potential spam or disruptive behavior.
