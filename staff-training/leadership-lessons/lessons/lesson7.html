The Staff Accountability & Improvement Process (SAIP) is a framework for guiding staff through challenges with fairness and professionalism. As a leader, your role is pivotal in ensuring consistency, growth, and support within your team.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Model Accountability: Demonstrate transparency and responsibility in your actions.
  • Foster Growth: Use challenges as opportunities for staff development.
  • Act with Integrity: Enforce the SAIP process fairly across all team members.
  • Offer Support: Provide mentorship and guidance to help staff succeed.

The SAIP system includes four tiers (T1-T4) for addressing staff issues:

  • T1: Informal coaching and correction for minor issues.
  • T2: Formal warning and improvement plan for repeated or moderate issues.
  • T3: Probation and leadership review for continued or significant offenses.
  • T4: Staff removal for extreme misconduct or failure to improve.

Effective leadership during the SAIP process requires key qualities:

  • Transparency & Integrity: Be open and honest with your team.
  • Patience & Empathy: Understand that change takes time and support growth.
  • Accountability & Fairness: Uphold the same standards for all team members.
  • Communication & Support: Regularly check in with staff and provide guidance.
