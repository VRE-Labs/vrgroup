🌟 Leadership Refresher Module

“Leadership isn’t a role—it’s a rhythm. Let’s fine-tune yours.”

📌 Module Objective

To refresh and reinforce leadership fundamentals with a focus on Accountability, Proactivity, Team Communication, and Community Trust.

This module is perfect for leadership recalibration after an incident, during onboarding, or as a quarterly refresher.

🧠 Section 1: The Four Pillars of Leadership

“A good leader knows the path. A great one walks it. An unforgettable one lights it for others.”

🧩 1. Accountability

  • You’re not responsible for perfection—just presence and ownership.
  • If something goes wrong under your watch, lead the post-mortem—not the excuse parade.
  • Own the L, learn from it, and lead with transparency.

💬 Prompt: “Think of a time where a delay or miscommunication happened. Did you address it clearly? What could have been done differently?”

⚙️ 2. Proactivity

  • Don’t wait for problems to happen—anticipate them.
  • Confirm roles, test setups, and always have a Plan B (and a person B).
  • Make use of event calendars, reminders, and written expectations.

📌 Key Tip: Use time blocking on your calendar to plan pre-event checks and team confirmations.

🗣️ 3. Team Communication

  • Leadership doesn’t mean soloing—it means delegating and informing.
  • Tag team members, confirm responsibilities, and always assume nothing has been seen until it’s confirmed.
  • Your team doesn’t follow what you think—they follow what you say.

💡 Best Practice: After assigning roles, drop a checklist message:

✅ Ares (Lead Host)
✅ Skye (Backup Host)
✅ Lys (Mod)
“Everyone good to go? Ping me if issues!”

🤝 4. Community Trust

  • Your consistency sets the tone. If you’re late, disengaged, or scattered, the community feels that.
  • Reinforce your reliability by being the calm in the storm.
  • Communicate early and clearly when changes occur.

🔊 Phrase to Remember: “Clarity is kindness. Silence is chaos.”

🔁 Section 2: The Event Lifecycle Checklist

  • Pre-Event (24–48 Hours Prior):
    • Finalized time/date listed on calendar & Discord
    • Confirmed co-hosts & mods
    • Prepped materials/worlds/media
    • Backups alerted
    • Reminder post sent
  • Day Of (1–2 Hours Prior):
    • Post hype ping or story
    • Test world/tech setup
    • Confirm backup presence
    • Load in 15 mins early
    • Send final “event starts soon!” message
  • Post-Event:
    • Thank attendees
    • Submit F.L.A.R.E. report
    • Acknowledge mishaps (if applicable)
    • Record highlights for repeat events or community reels
    • Offer feedback privately to co-hosts/staff

✨ Section 3: Rebuilding After a Miss

If something goes wrong:

  • Acknowledge publicly:
    • “Hey folks! Last night’s event had a delay due to some timing missteps on our end. That’s on us—and we’re tightening up our pre-event coordination for the future.”
  • Debrief internally. Don’t wait for a crisis to start a conversation.
  • Reflect in the SPARK logs or a leadership journal.
  • Apply a small, clear change (e.g., backup host confirmation form, 15-min prep rule).

🗓️ Optional Challenge: The Leadership Pulse

Each leadership member should set a personal “pulse check” once every two weeks. Ask yourself:

  • Have I been proactive, or reactive?
  • Have I supported my team?
  • Have I followed through on what I promised?
